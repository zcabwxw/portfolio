When to wash your hands

  • Before eating, feeding, or preparing food. This prevents germs from getting into the mouth from hands.
  • After they have touched saliva (after feeding or eating), mucous (wiping a nose, using a tissue), bodily fluids (toileting, diapering), food, or animals.
  • When visibly dirty, such as after touching garbage or after cleaning.
  • Before cooking.
  • In between preparing meats and vegetables.
