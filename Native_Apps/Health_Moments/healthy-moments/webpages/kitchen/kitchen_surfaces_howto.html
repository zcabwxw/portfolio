How to wash surfaces

  • How to wash surfaces depends on what type of cleaning you will do. The labels on cleaning products provide instructions on how to use the product appropriately for different uses.
  • Cleaning removes visible soil, dirt, and most germs. Soap and water will clean most surfaces.
  • Sanitizing reduces germs to a level that is unlikely to cause disease, but does not totally get rid of them. You may want to sanitize surfaces where you eat (such as a table or high chair tray) and toys that children place in their mouths. It is important to follow the instructions on the label. This may also include rinsing surfaces/toys after using the sanitizing product to remove any remaining chemicals.
  • Disinfecting destroys or inactivates infectious germs on surfaces. Disinfectants may be used on diaper-changing tables, toilets, and counter tops. Be sure to follow the instructions on the product label.10
