Learning activities

Handwashing can be fun. Most children love to play in water. They usually enjoy making bubbles as they mix water and soap, which can make washing hands a fun experience. While washing hands, parents can sing to young children or count their fingers. Older children can sing or rhyme, count, and practice their independent skills by washing their hands.

Germ Handprints. Use household flour or cornstarch as “germs.” Sprinkle some flour on a table and have the child or parent touch the “germs." Then, watch how the germs spread.

  • Try this in the bathroom. Touch the flusher. Then touch the faucet, bathtub, etc.
  • Try this in the kitchen. Touch the counter. Then touch the faucet, cabinet handles, etc.
  • Explain that washing the germs off your hands prevents the germs from spreading to surfaces in your home.
