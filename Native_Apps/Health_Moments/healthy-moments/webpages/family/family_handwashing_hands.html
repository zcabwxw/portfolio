When to wash your hands

  • Before eating, feeding, or preparing food. This prevents germs on your hands from getting into your mouth.
  • After touching saliva (after feeding or eating), mucous (wiping a nose, using a tissue), bodily fluids (toileting, diapering), food, or animals.
  • When visibly dirty, such as after touching garbage or after cleaning.3
