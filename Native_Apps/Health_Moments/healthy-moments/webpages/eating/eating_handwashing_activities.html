Learning activities

Play food. Bring some play food on the home visit and have a pretend meal with a toddler or preschool child and his parent. Then talk about how handwashing before eating helps to keep our bodies healthy.

Handwashing can be fun. Most children love to play in water. They usually enjoy making bubbles as they mix water and soap, which can make washing hands a fun experience. While washing hands, parents can sing to young children or count their fingers. Older children can sing or rhyme, count, and practice their independent skills.

Germ Handprints. Use household flour or cornstarch as “germs.” Sprinkle some flour on a table and have the child or parent touch the “germs”; then watch how the germs spread.

  • Try this in the bathroom. Touch the flusher; then touch the faucet, bathtub, etc.
  • Try this in the kitchen. Sprinkle on the counter, touch it, and then touch the faucet, cabinet handles, etc.
  • Share that washing the germs off your hands prevents the germs from spreading to surfaces in your home.
